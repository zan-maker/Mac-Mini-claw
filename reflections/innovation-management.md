## Initial Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: The 5-Phase Innovation Cycle
1. **Scanning:** Systematic opportunity identification
2. **Generation:** Creative idea development
3. **Evaluation:** Rigorous assessment and selection
4. **Implementation:** Effective execution and delivery
5. **Learning:** Systematic reflection and improvement

### Hypothesis 2: Innovation Type Classification
Based on Proactive Value Creation framework:
- **Incremental Innovation:** Small improvements to existing systems
- **Architectural Innovation:** New approaches to existing problems
- **Disruptive Innovation:** New solutions to new or existing problems
- **Radical Innovation:** Fundamental changes to systems or approaches

### Hypothesis 3: The Innovation Success Framework
1. **Value Creation:** Innovation delivers measurable value
2. **Feasibility:** Innovation can be implemented with available resources
3. **Acceptance:** Innovation is adopted by stakeholders
4. **Sustainability:** Innovation creates lasting value
5. **Scalability:** Innovation can grow and evolve over time

### Hypothesis 4: Innovation Risk-Reward Assessment
- **High Risk, High Reward:** Radical innovations, new markets, major changes
- **Medium Risk, Medium Reward:** Architectural innovations, significant improvements
- **Low Risk, Low Reward:** Incremental innovations, small improvements
- **Low Risk, High Reward:** "Blue ocean" opportunities (rare but valuable)
