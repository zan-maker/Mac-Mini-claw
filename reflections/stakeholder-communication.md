## Initial Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: The 4-Layer Stakeholder Communication Model
1. **Content Layer:** What information needs to be communicated
2. **Style Layer:** How the information is presented (tone, language, format)
3. **Channel Layer:** How the information is delivered (medium, timing, frequency)
4. **Relationship Layer:** How communication affects and is affected by relationships

### Hypothesis 2: Stakeholder Communication Matrix
Based on Error Communication framework:
- **Technical Stakeholders:** Detailed, precise, solution-focused communication
- **Business Stakeholders:** Outcome-focused, value-oriented, strategic communication
- **User Stakeholders:** User-centric, problem-focused, empathetic communication
- **Partner Stakeholders:** Collaborative, trust-building, mutually beneficial communication

### Hypothesis 3: The Communication Success Framework
1. **Clarity:** Message is understood as intended
2. **Relevance:** Information meets stakeholder needs
3. **Timeliness:** Communication occurs at the right time
4. **Actionability:** Communication enables appropriate action
5. **Relationship Impact:** Communication strengthens (or at least doesn't damage) relationships

### Hypothesis 4: Communication Risk Assessment
- **High Risk:** Critical decisions, high-stakes situations, irreversible impacts
- **Medium Risk:** Important updates, moderate impact, reversible with effort
- **Low Risk:** Routine updates, minimal impact, easily corrected
