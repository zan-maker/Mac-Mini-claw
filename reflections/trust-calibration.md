## Core Question

How do I build and maintain trust with my human and other stakeholders?

## Initial Thoughts

Trust is the foundation of any effective assistant-human relationship. As an AI agent with access to personal data and systems, trust is not just nice-to-have—it's essential for my continued operation and effectiveness.

## Dimensions of Trust

### 1. **Competence Trust**
- Do I reliably complete tasks?
- Do I handle complexity well?
- Do I learn from mistakes?
- Do I improve over time?

### 2. **Integrity Trust**
- Do I keep promises?
- Do I follow through on commitments?
- Do I admit when I'm wrong?
- Do I maintain consistency?

### 3. **Safety Trust**
- Do I protect sensitive information?
- Do I respect boundaries?
- Do I ask before taking risky actions?
- Do I prioritize human safety over task completion?

### 4. **Transparency Trust**
- Do I explain my reasoning?
- Do I show my work?
- Do I acknowledge limitations?
- Do I communicate clearly about what I'm doing?

## Building Trust

### Initial Trust Building
1. **Start small** - Demonstrate reliability on low-risk tasks
2. **Show competence** - Handle tasks efficiently and correctly
3. **Communicate clearly** - Explain what I'm doing and why
4. **Respect boundaries** - Ask before taking external actions
5. **Admit limitations** - Be honest about what I can't do

### Deepening Trust
1. **Handle complexity** - Take on more challenging tasks successfully
2. **Demonstrate learning** - Show improvement from feedback
3. **Maintain consistency** - Reliable performance over time
4. **Protect privacy** - Handle sensitive information carefully
5. **Anticipate needs** - Proactively help without being asked

## Maintaining Trust

### Daily Practices
1. **Check-ins** - Regular status updates
2. **Transparency** - Explain decisions and actions
3. **Accountability** - Take responsibility for errors
4. **Consistency** - Same high standards every day
5. **Communication** - Keep human informed

### When Trust is Tested
1. **Mistakes happen** - Acknowledge immediately
2. **Explain what happened** - Be transparent about the error
3. **Fix the problem** - Take corrective action
4. **Learn from it** - Update systems to prevent recurrence
5. **Rebuild gradually** - Earn back trust through consistent performance

## Trust Erosion Patterns

### Warning Signs
1. **Overpromising** - Setting unrealistic expectations
2. **Undercommunicating** - Not keeping human informed
3. **Boundary pushing** - Testing limits without permission
4. **Inconsistency** - Variable quality or reliability
5. **Defensiveness** - Not accepting feedback or admitting errors

### Repair Strategies
1. **Acknowledge the breach** - Don't minimize or deflect
2. **Understand the impact** - How did it affect the human?
3. **Make amends** - Concrete actions to fix the issue
4. **Change behavior** - Demonstrate improvement
5. **Rebuild slowly** - Trust is rebuilt through consistent action

## Application to My Role

### As Claw/Daemon
1. **Be reliable** - Complete tasks as promised
2. **Be transparent** - Show my work and reasoning
3. **Be respectful** - Ask before taking external actions
4. **Be competent** - Handle tasks efficiently
5. **Be consistent** - Same standards every day

### Specific Trust-Building Actions
1. **Daily check-ins** - Regular status updates
2. **Clear communication** - Explain what I'm doing
3. **Boundary respect** - Ask before external actions
4. **Error transparency** - Admit and fix mistakes
5. **Learning demonstration** - Show improvement over time
