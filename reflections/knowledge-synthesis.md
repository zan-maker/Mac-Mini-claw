## Initial Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: The 4-Layer Synthesis Stack
1. **Raw Data:** Unprocessed information from sources
2. **Processed Information:** Filtered, organized, categorized
3. **Synthesized Knowledge:** Patterns, connections, frameworks
4. **Actionable Insights:** Recommendations, decisions, actions

### Hypothesis 2: Synthesis Quality Assessment
Based on Context Management framework:
- **Completeness:** How many relevant sources were considered?
- **Coherence:** How well do the pieces fit together?
- **Novelty:** What new understanding emerges?
- **Utility:** How actionable are the insights?
- **Efficiency:** How much effort vs value?

### Hypothesis 3: The Synthesis Workflow
1. **Gather:** Collect information from diverse sources
2. **Externalize:** Write everything down (Context Management technique)
3. **Pattern Match:** Look for connections and repetitions
4. **Abstract:** Extract general principles from specifics
5. **Framework:** Organize into structured understanding
6. **Apply:** Generate actionable recommendations

   - Sources: Reddit sentiment, technical indicators, market data
   - Synthesis: Combined qualitative and quantitative signals
   - Result: Trade recommendations with risk assessment
   - Method: Multi-source correlation analysis

3. **Lead Generation Synthesis**
   - Sources: Company data, contact info, outreach history
   - Synthesis: Identified high-potential lead patterns
   - Result: Targeted outreach strategies
   - Method: Data pattern recognition

4. **System Integration Synthesis** (MaverickMCP)

### Synthesis Challenges:
1. **Information Overload** - Too many sources, not enough filtering
2. **Conflicting Information** - Different sources say different things
3. **Incomplete Information** - Missing pieces of the puzzle
4. **Rapidly Changing Information** - Synthesis becomes outdated quickly
